Abstract Translation: NDSS 2015 CopperDroid

论文基本信息

作者
Tam, Kimberly, Salahuddin J. Khan, Aristide Fattori, and Lorenzo Cavallaro.
标题
CopperDroid: Automatic Reconstruction of Android Malware Behaviors.
发表于
NDSS. 2015.

摘要翻译

Mobile devices and their application marketplaces drive the entire economy of the today’s mobile landscape.

移动设备和移动应用市场驱动了今天整个移动行业经济的发展。

Android platforms alone have produced staggering revenues, exceeding ﬁve billion USD, which has attracted cybercriminals and increased malware in Android markets at an alarming rate.

仅 Android 平台自身就创造了超过 50 亿美金的令人难以置信的收益。但这也吸引了网络犯罪，Android 市场上恶意应用正在以令人担忧的速率快速增加。

  • staggering, so great, shocking or surprising that it is difficult to believe 令人难以相信的

To better understand this slew of threats, we present CopperDroid, an automatic VMI-based dynamic analysis system to reconstruct the behaviors of Android malware.

为了深入理解 Android 平台面临的恶意应用威胁，本文提出了 CopperDroid, 一种基于虚拟机自省技术(VMI)的自动化动态分析系统，用来重建 Android 恶意应用的行为.

The novelty of CopperDroid lies in its agnostic approach to identify interesting OS- and high-level Android-speciﬁc behaviors.

CopperDroid 的创新之处在于，他采用了一种（底层）不可知的方法来发现（恶意应用中）值得关注的 OS 层行为和 Android 特有的高层行为。

It reconstructs these behaviors by observing and dissecting system calls and, therefore, is resistant to the multitude of alterations the Android runtime is subjected to over its life-cycle.

通过监视并剖析系统调用，CopperDroid 就能够重建这些行为。（由于系统调用在 Android 运行时之下），因此，在 CopperDroid 的整个生命周期中，即使 Android 运行时发生多种变化，它都不受影响。

CopperDroid automatically and accurately reconstructs events of interest that describe, not only well-known process-OS interactions (e.g., ﬁle and process creation), but also complex intra- and inter-process communications (e.g., SMS reception), whose semantics are typically contextualized through complex Android objects.

CopperDroid 能自动并且准确地重建所关注的事件。这些事件不仅描述了常见的进程-操作系统交互（例如，文件和进程的创建），而且描述了复杂的进程内部和进程间通信（例如， SMS 接收）。一般来说，进程内和进程间通信事件的语义必须放在复杂的 Android 对象的上下文环境中考虑。

Because CopperDroid’s reconstruction mechanisms are agnostic to the underlying action invocation methods, it is able to capture actions initiated both from Java and native code execution.

由于 CopperDroid 的重建机制不会被下层活动的调用方法所感知，所以无论从 Java 发起的还是从本地代码中发起的行为，它都能捕获。

CopperDroid’s analysis generates detailed behavioral proﬁles that abstract a large stream of low-level—often uninteresting—events into concise, high-level semantics, which are well-suited to provide insightful behavioral traits and open the possibility to further research directions.

CopperDroid 的分析能将大量底层的——往往很无趣的——事件抽象成简洁的、高层次的语义描述，从而产生恶意应用的详细的行为特征轮廓。这种轮廓非常适合提供恶意应用的有洞察力的行为特点，而且给未来的研究方向带来更多可能。

We carried out an extensive evaluation to assess the capabilities and performance of CopperDroid on more than 2,900 Android malware samples.

我们在超过 2900 个 Android 恶意应用上作了大量测试，以评估 CopperDroid 的功能和性能。

Our experiments show that CopperDroid faithfully reconstructs OS- and Android-speciﬁc behaviors.

实验表明，CopperDroid 能准确地重建 OS 层行为和 Android 特有行为。

Additionally, we demonstrate how CopperDroid can be leveraged to disclose additional behaviors through the use of a simple, yet effective, app stimulation technique.

另外，通过使用一种简单但有效的 app 模拟技术，我们展示了如何借助 CopperDroid 暴露应用的更多行为。

Using this technique, we successfully triggered and disclosed additional behaviors on more than 60% of the analyzed malware samples.

利用这种技术，在所分析的应用中，我们成功在 60% 以上的应用中触发并暴露了额外行为。

This qualitatively demonstrates the versatility of CopperDroid’s ability to improve dynamic-based code coverage.

这定性地说明，CopperDroid 在提高动态分析代码覆盖率上也有一定作用。
